Security Logging and Alerting Failures

Security logging and monitoring failures occur when systems do not properly record, monitor, or respond to important events. This can include missing logs, insufficient detail, or failing to detect suspicious activity.


Why?

Without proper logging and monitoring, attacks can go unnoticed for long periods of time. Even if a system is compromised, a lack of visibility makes it difficult to detect, investigate, or respond to incidents. In some cases, exposed error messages or logs may also reveal useful information to attackers, aiding further exploitation.


How?

This vulnerability occurs when applications fail to log important events or do not monitor activity for suspicious behavior. To prevent this, systems should implement proper logging, monitor for unusual activity, and ensure sensitive information is not exposed in logs or error messages.
